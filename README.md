# Statistical-Analysis-of-Renewable-Energy-Adoption-using-R
# 📋 Overview 
The research investigates the effect of renewable eneregz adoption on the utilization of cleaner fossil fuels, fossil fuels and nuclear energy in 20 major energy
consuming countries: Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Iran, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Korea, Spain, Thailand, 
Turkey and United Kingdom from 1990 to 2022. 

# 📌 Primary Research Objective
- To assess the impact of renewable energy consumption on the consumption of traditional fossil fuels, cleaner fossil fuels and nuclear energy.

# 📌 Secondary Research Objectives
- To determine the effect of renewable energy consumption on coal consumption.
- To meaure the impact of renewable energy consumption on oil consumption.
- To investigate the influence of renewable consumption on natural gas consumption.
- To evaluate the relationship between renewable energy consumption and nuclear energy consumption. 

# 📈 Analysis Workflow 
- Imported and inspected secondary datasets from the World Bank.
- Computed descriptive statisctics and visualized data.
- Computed summary statisctics and regression analyisis.
- Conducted diagnostics tests.
- Interpreted statistical results.
- Reported findings and conclusions.

# 🔍 Example Result 1
- Null hypothesis (H0): There is no significant impact of renewable energy consumption on oil consumption.
- Alternative hypothesis (H1): Renewable energy consumption significantly impacts oil consumption. 

After performing  t-test:
- p-value > 0.05 -> Accept H0
- Conclusion: It can be suggested that trade has no explanatory power on M4 and Trade has a non-significant effect on GDP, holding all other variables constant. 

# 🔍 Example Result 2
- Null hypothesis (H0): There is no significant influence of renewable energy consumption on natural gas consumption
- Alternative hypothesis (H1): Renewable energy consumption significantly influences natural gas consumption.

After performing t-test:
- p-value < 0.05 -> Reject H0
- Conclusion: M8 significantly affects GDP suggesting that Model M8 has the largest explanatory power for economic development, holding all other variables constant.
